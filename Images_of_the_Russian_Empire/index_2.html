CS180 Project 1 — Colorizing the Prokudin-Gorskii Collection

Each scene has three plates (B, G, R) taken with different color filters. The task is to align the channels and composite a color image. Each scene has three grayscale plates taken with different color filters. With proper alignment and compositing, we can render a color image.

1) Single-Scale Alignment (SSD/L2) 3 Images

Approach

I align the R and G channels to B by brute-force search over a range of integer displacements \((shiftx, shifty)\in[-15,15]\). Before scoring, I crop a harcoded amount of 35 pixels off all sides to avoid border artifacts. The score is the **L2/SSD** between brightness-normalized patches: 

SSD(ImgA, ImgB; shiftx,shifty) = Σ (ImgA_crop(x,y) - ImgB_crop(x+shiftx, y+shifty))²

I choose the displacement minimizing SSD for each channel, then stack as [R*, G*, B]. This baseline works well for the smaller JPGs due to the low-res nature of the images.

Key Details

  • Search window: [-15, 15] px
  • Reference: Blue channel (B)
  • Border crop (hard coded 35 pixels each border)
  • Output: displacements per channel + visual inspection

2) Multi-Scale Pyramid Alignment (NCC / L2)

Motivation

High-resolution TIFs make brute-force search too slow and sensitive to local optima. We cannot efficiently hardcode a searchable range of pixels for images as large as we are dealing with. I use a **Gaussian image pyramid** (coarse→fine) with NCC scoring. At coarse levels, large motions become small; I refine displacements as I upsample back to full resolution. NCC Scoring takes the dot product between two normalized vectors. These vectors represent rows of pixels that we can check the difference between. We are trying to maximize NCC. In addition, I alter the image metric that we are trying to NCC maximize. I switch from using Pixel Brightness to using Sobel Edge detection. The Sobel method computes the image-intensity gradient by convolving over the individual color channel in the x and then y direction, computing the derivatives and uses the gradient magnitude for robust matching and accurate alignment.
